ABERRATION DOCUMENT: ABR-007

Aberration Designation: ABR-007 Luminous Halo

Threat Level: Class II: Controllable

Containment Level: Zeta-1

First image of ABR-007

Description

ABR-007, known as the "Luminous Halo," is an anomalous atmospheric phenomenon characterized by a large, bright neon sky blue ring suspended within cloud formations. This ring measures approximately 500 meters in diameter and remains stationary at an altitude of 1,000 meters above ground level. Notably, it exclusively appears in isolated rural regions, avoiding urban environments.

First identified on [DATE] in [LOCATION], [COUNTRY], ABR-007 was reported by multiple witnesses who described an intense neon blue ring visible during night hours. ARI field agents confirmed the anomaly's existence and administered amnestic treatments to the witnesses, followed by the dissemination of misinformation to prevent further public inquiries.

Containment Protocols

  • Remote observation stations must be established within a 20-kilometer radius of any reported occurrence of ABR-007. These stations are to record and analyze the phenomenon discreetly.
  • Data collection should utilize encrypted channels to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Personnel at observation posts are required to undergo standard psychological evaluations to ensure their ability to handle the psychological impact of ABR-007.
  • Unauthorized civilian witnesses are to be subjected to Class-C amnestic administration as per protocol.

Risk Assessment

ABR-007 presents minimal danger to both personnel and the public. Its presence enhances nocturnal visibility due to its consistent phosphorescent glow. While it has demonstrated the capacity to influence local weather conditions slightly, these effects are not significant enough to pose any substantial threat. The anomaly's preference for isolated rural areas suggests an aversion to densely populated regions, possibly reducing its potential impact on human populations.

Behavioral Analysis

ABR-007's behavior includes maintaining a fixed position within cloud formations for periods ranging from several hours to days before disappearing abruptly without leaving residues or atmospheric disturbances. Researchers hypothesize that the anomaly's avoidance of urban areas might be due to factors such as urban light pollution or other yet-to-be-identified variables.
