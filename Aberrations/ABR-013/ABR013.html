ABERRATION DOCUMENT: ABR-013 "The Resonant Metronome"

Aberration Designation: ABR-013 "The Resonant Metronome"

Threat Level: Class III: Hazardous

Containment Level: Gamma-1

Description

ABR-013, also known as "The Resonant Metronome," appears to be an ordinary metronome, standing approximately 24 cm tall, with a polished mahogany casing. It lacks any identifiable manufacturer's mark. ABR-013's anomalous properties manifest when its pendulum begins to swing, which occurs at unpredictable intervals and without any apparent external stimulus.

When ABR-013 begins its motion, it generates a rhythmic sound, typical of a metronome, but at an unusually high frequency. This sound wave has the anomalous ability to vibrate and manipulate matter at a molecular level. The strength and effects of the manipulation are proportional to the proximity and duration of exposure to ABR-013.

The manifestations of ABR-013’s effects include but are not limited to:

  • Softening or hardening of materials.
  • Alteration of the chemical structure of compounds.
  • Manipulation of physical states of matter (solid, liquid, gas, and plasma).
  • Inducing or halting kinetic motion within inanimate objects.

The frequency and rhythm of ABR-013's ticking do not seem to correlate with the specific effects it induces, suggesting that the exact outcome of exposure may be determined by factors not yet understood.

Containment Protocols

ABR-013 is to be contained within a 5x5x5 meter containment unit at ARI Site-77. The containment unit should be constructed from reinforced steel and equipped with high-frequency sound-proofing technology.

No personnel should come within 2 meters of ABR-013 unless wearing proper protective gear, consisting of noise-canceling headphones and full-body suits designed to resist high-frequency vibration.

In the event of a containment breach, the sector housing ABR-013 is to be locked down and flooded with low-frequency sound waves until ABR-013 becomes inactive.

Risk Assessment

ABR-013 presents a significant hazard due to its ability to manipulate matter at a molecular level. Uncontrolled exposure can lead to structural failures, chemical hazards, and unpredictable physical transformations. The unpredictable nature of its activation and effects necessitates stringent containment protocols and rapid response measures to mitigate potential breaches.

Behavioral Analysis

ABR-013's activation is sporadic and not influenced by any discernible external stimuli. The duration and intensity of its effects vary, further complicating containment and management efforts. Analysis suggests that while ABR-013 can affect a wide range of materials and states of matter, the outcomes are not consistent, indicating a complex and poorly understood mechanism behind its anomalous properties.

Appendices

Background: ABR-013 was discovered in ██████, Austria, after a series of unusual incidents in a local music school. Reports of instruments behaving anomalously, including pianos playing themselves, violins strings vibrating uncontrollably, and brass instruments reshaping during performances, led to an undercover ARI investigation. The object was found in the office of the school's principal, who had reportedly been gifted the metronome by an anonymous donor. The principal was administered Class-B amnestics, and ABR-013 was taken into ARI custody.

Incident Reports:

  • Incident 013-A: During routine testing, ABR-013 activated and caused the containment unit's steel walls to soften, resulting in a temporary breach. Emergency protocols were enacted, and the unit was reinforced with additional sound-proofing.
  • Incident 013-B: Exposure to ABR-013 resulted in the spontaneous liquefaction of a containment specialist's tools. The specialist was unharmed due to the protective gear but reported severe disorientation due to the high-frequency vibrations.

Research Notes:

  • Experiment 013-01: Testing ABR-013's effects on various materials showed that organic matter is less susceptible to immediate changes but prolonged exposure leads to molecular destabilization.
  • Experiment 013-02: Attempts to correlate the frequency of ABR-013's ticking with specific outcomes have been inconclusive, indicating a need for further study into the underlying principles of its anomalous behavior.

Proposal for Reclassification: Due to the potential utility of ABR-013 in altering the properties of other Aberrations, some researchers have proposed upgrading ABR-013 to a higher classification, allowing for its use in controlled experiments. This proposal is currently under review by the Executive Council.
