E-Class Personnel Overview

Designation: E-Class Personnel

Role: High-Risk Experimentation and Testing

Clearance Level: E-Class - Expendable

Description

E-Class Personnel, often referred to as "expendables," are individuals assigned to high-risk experiments and testing within the Aberration Research Institute (ARI). These personnel are typically sourced from individuals who have volunteered for high-risk duties, or those who have been assigned to E-Class roles due to terminal conditions, life sentences, or other circumstances that align with the ethical standards set by the ARI Ethical Review Board.

Selection Criteria

Selection for E-Class Personnel is rigorous and follows strict ethical guidelines:

  • Voluntary Participation: Preference is given to individuals who volunteer for E-Class duties. Volunteers are fully briefed on the risks and nature of the experiments.
  • Medical and Psychological Evaluation: All E-Class candidates undergo thorough medical and psychological evaluations to ensure they are fit for the high-risk nature of the tasks.
  • Ethical Oversight: Assignments to E-Class roles are reviewed and approved by the ARI Ethical Review Board to ensure compliance with ethical standards and the well-being of the personnel involved.

Roles and Responsibilities

E-Class Personnel are involved in the following activities:

  • High-Risk Experimentation: Participating in experiments with high-risk aberrations that could pose significant danger to life or health.
  • Testing of Anomalous Objects: Handling and testing anomalous objects to gather data on their properties and effects.
  • Containment Protocols: Assisting in the development and testing of containment protocols for new or particularly dangerous aberrations.

Safety and Support

Despite the high-risk nature of their duties, E-Class Personnel are provided with comprehensive safety measures and support:

  • Protective Equipment: Advanced protective gear and monitoring devices to ensure their safety during experiments.
  • Medical Support: Immediate access to medical care in case of injuries or adverse effects during testing.
  • Psychological Support: Regular psychological evaluations and support to address the mental stress associated with high-risk tasks.

Incident Reports and Case Studies

E-Class Personnel are often the subjects of detailed incident reports and case studies, which help improve safety protocols and enhance the understanding of aberrations. These reports are crucial for advancing the research goals of the ARI while ensuring continuous improvement in personnel safety and ethical standards.

Career Progression

While E-Class roles are inherently high-risk, ARI provides pathways for career progression and transition into other roles within the organization. Successful E-Class Personnel who demonstrate exceptional resilience and capability may be considered for transfer to less hazardous positions, with opportunities for further training and development.

Ethical Considerations

All activities involving E-Class Personnel are conducted under strict ethical guidelines. The ARI Ethical Review Board regularly reviews the assignments and ensures that the rights, well-being, and dignity of E-Class Personnel are respected and protected at all times. Informed consent, transparency, and voluntary participation are core principles upheld throughout the selection and operational processes.
