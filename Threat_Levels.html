ARI Aberration Threat Level Classification

Class 0: Beneficial

Description: Anomalies with positive effects on the environment, health, or well-being. Require minimal containment.

Class I: Harmless

Description: No threat to personnel or the public. Effects are negligible and require minimal observation.

Class II: Inconsequential

Description: Pose minimal to no threat. Effects are benign and easily managed.

Class III: Controllable

Description: Cause minor disruptions or localized effects but are effectively controlled with standard protocols.

Class IV: Hazardous

Description: Present moderate danger. Can harm personnel, infrastructure, or the environment if mishandled.

Class V: Critical

Description: Highly dangerous with potential catastrophic consequences. Threaten life, property, or reality's integrity.

Class VI: Apocalyptic

Description: Existential threat to humanity and the cosmos. Capable of causing global or cosmic devastation.

Class VII: Unknown

Description: Defy current understanding and present unpredictable danger. Properties and potential effects are not fully known.

Class VIII: Reality-Altering

Description: Capable of altering or unraveling the fabric of reality itself. Threatens the existence of the universe.
