ARI Security Clearance Rank List

The Aberration Research Institute (ARI) employs a strict hierarchy of security clearance levels to ensure that sensitive information is accessed only by authorized personnel. Below is an in-depth look at each clearance level and the roles associated with them.

Clearance Level 6 (C6 - Censored and Unknown)

Description: The highest security level, known only to select high-ranking officials. Information at this level is strictly compartmentalized to protect the most sensitive and classified data.

  • Access: Top-secret projects, inter-dimensional travel data, high-threat aberration details.
  • Roles: ARI's top echelon including the Director and trusted advisors.
  • Responsibilities: Oversee the institute's most critical and sensitive operations.

Clearance Level 5 (C5 - Executive Council)

Description: Executive Council members oversee critical decisions, classified information, and top-level initiatives. They are pivotal in strategic planning and resource allocation.

  • Access: Comprehensive operational data, high-level policy documents, inter-departmental coordination.
  • Roles: Senior executives, head strategists.
  • Responsibilities: Formulate and implement high-impact projects and policies.

Clearance Level 4 (C4 - Division Heads)

Description: Division Heads control departmental information, vital research data, and containment protocols. They ensure effective research and containment strategies within their departments.

  • Access: Department-specific data, sensitive project information, advanced containment protocols.
  • Roles: Heads of research, containment, and operational divisions.
  • Responsibilities: Manage departmental operations and personnel.

Clearance Level 3 (C3 - Lead Researchers and Containment Specialists)

Description: Given to lead researchers, containment specialists, and agents. This level involves access to detailed operational information and sensitive data necessary for their specialized roles.

  • Access: Detailed research data, containment plans, and operational protocols.
  • Roles: Senior researchers, lead containment specialists, field agents.
  • Responsibilities: Conduct research, develop and implement containment strategies, handle field operations.

Clearance Level 2 (C2 - Support Personnel)

Description: Assigned to support staff including administration, analysts, security, and tech support. Access is restricted to information necessary for their tasks.

  • Access: Operational support data, administrative records, general security information.
  • Roles: Administrative staff, data analysts, security personnel, technical support.
  • Responsibilities: Provide essential services to support ARI's operations.

Clearance Level 1 (C1 - General Personnel)

Description: Covers general personnel with access to basic information required for routine operations. These individuals do not handle sensitive data.

  • Access: Basic operational information, non-sensitive data.
  • Roles: Maintenance staff, general operational personnel.
  • Responsibilities: Perform day-to-day tasks and support roles within ARI.

Clearance Level 0 (C0 - Non-essential Personnel)

Description: Limited access to critical information. Includes expendable personnel such as death row convicts, terminally ill individuals, and volunteers.

  • Access: Highly restricted, minimal critical information.
  • Roles: Volunteers, non-critical support roles.
  • Responsibilities: Engage in specific tasks with minimal risk exposure, under strict ethical review.

Personnel Advancement and Training

Clearance levels can be adjusted based on performance, training, and qualifications. ARI provides a clear career progression path with regular reviews and targeted training to handle higher responsibilities.

  • Advancement: Performance reviews, additional qualifications, and demonstrated competencies can lead to clearance upgrades.
  • Training: Comprehensive programs covering aberration research, containment protocols, emergency response, and ethical considerations.
