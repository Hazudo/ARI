Classification of Containment Levels

Α α - Alpha (Benign)

  • Alpha-1: Non-threatening, easily contained, or beneficial.
  • Examples: Docile plants with unique properties (e.g., glow-in-the-dark flowers used for illumination).
  • Alpha-2: Slightly unpredictable but generally harmless.
  • Examples: Communicating plants that respond to human emotions by changing colors.

Β β - Beta (Low Risk)

  • Beta-1: Minor containment required; low risk.
  • Examples: Small animals with non-harmful anomalous traits.
  • Beta-2: Unusual but not hazardous; basic precautions needed.
  • Examples: Objects that change shape but pose no danger.

Γ γ - Gamma (Moderate Risk)

  • Gamma-1: Require specific containment measures, moderate risk.
  • Examples: Telepathic animals capable of basic mind reading.
  • Gamma-2: Unpredictable or partially understood; potential danger if improperly contained.
  • Examples: Shape-shifting materials that can alter their form based on environmental stimuli.
  • Gamma-3: Intentional or malevolent behavior; robust containment needed.
  • Examples: Sentient AI with the potential to override control systems.

Δ δ - Delta (High Risk)

  • Delta-1: Highly dangerous but containable with advanced measures.
  • Examples: Controlled black holes used for research but needing constant monitoring.
  • Delta-2: Exert control or influence over objects, space, or personnel.
  • Examples: Mind-controlling parasites capable of influencing host behavior.
  • Delta-3: Potential widespread damage or catastrophe if containment fails.
  • Examples: Uncontrollable weather machines that can create severe weather patterns.

Ε ε - Epsilon (Extreme Danger)

  • Epsilon-1: Imminent threat to ARI, local populations, or global stability.
  • Examples: Time manipulation devices that can alter historical events.
  • Epsilon-2: Nearly impossible to contain due to immense power or chaotic behavior.
  • Examples: Interdimensional portals that can randomly open and close, risking cross-dimensional invasions.
  • Epsilon-3: Potential bioweapon or mass-destruction capabilities; top-priority containment.
  • Examples: Bio-engineered viruses designed for rapid and lethal outbreaks.

Ζ ζ - Zeta (Special Cases)

  • Zeta-1: Require unconventional or experimental containment methods.
  • Examples: Living paintings that can trap people within their scenes.
  • Zeta-2: Symbiotic or intertwined with key personnel, technology, or other Aberrations.
  • Examples: Soul-bonded artifacts that require a human host to function.
  • Zeta-3: Involving alien technology or cosmic phenomena.
  • Examples: Extraterrestrial devices with unknown functions and potentially catastrophic capabilities.

Η η - Eta (Uncontained)

  • Eta-1: Known and monitored but currently uncontainable due to resource or technological limitations.
  • Examples: Wandering spectral entities that appear sporadically and unpredictably.
  • Eta-2: Actively evading containment efforts, posing significant threat.
  • Examples: Rogue telekinetic individuals who have escaped containment.
  • Eta-3: Unknown whereabouts; high priority for retrieval and containment.
  • Examples: A sentient nanobot swarm that escaped during a research project.

Θ θ - Theta (Experimental)

  • Theta-1: Require specific containment strategies due to their unique properties.
  • Examples: Entities that exhibit quantum tunneling or phasing abilities.
  • Theta-2: Demonstrate anomalous interactions with known physical laws.
  • Examples: Objects that defy gravity or thermodynamics.
  • Theta-3: Exhibit behavior or properties that are not yet fully understood or classified.
  • Examples: Phenomena that alter time perception or cause temporal anomalies.

Ι ι - Iota (Psychological)

  • Iota-1: Interact with or manipulate cognitive functions or perceptions.
  • Examples: Memetic entities that influence thoughts or behaviors.
  • Iota-2: Capable of inducing psychological or emotional changes in individuals.
  • Examples: Artifacts that evoke intense fear or euphoria.
  • Iota-3: Pose significant risks due to their ability to affect large populations mentally.
  • Examples: Devices that broadcast subliminal messages or induce mass hysteria.
