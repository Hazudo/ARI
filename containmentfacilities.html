Classification of Containment Levels

Level Alpha - Benign

  • Alpha-1: Non-threatening, easily contained, or beneficial.
  • Examples: Docile plants with unique properties (e.g., glow-in-the-dark flowers used for illumination).
  • Alpha-2: Slightly unpredictable but generally harmless.
  • Examples: Communicating plants that respond to human emotions by changing colors.

Level Beta - Moderate Risk

  • Beta-1: Require specific containment measures, moderate risk.
  • Examples: Telepathic animals capable of basic mind reading.
  • Beta-2: Unpredictable or partially understood; potential danger if improperly contained.
  • Examples: Shape-shifting materials that can alter their form based on environmental stimuli.
  • Beta-3: Intentional or malevolent behavior; robust containment needed.
  • Examples: Sentient AI with the potential to override control systems.

Level Gamma - High Risk

  • Gamma-1: Highly dangerous but containable with advanced measures.
  • Examples: Controlled black holes used for research but needing constant monitoring.
  • Gamma-2: Exert control or influence over objects, space, or personnel.
  • Examples: Mind-controlling parasites capable of influencing host behavior.
  • Gamma-3: Potential widespread damage or catastrophe if containment fails.
  • Examples: Uncontrollable weather machines that can create severe weather patterns.

Level Delta - Extreme Danger

  • Delta-1: Imminent threat to ARI, local populations, or global stability.
  • Examples: Time manipulation devices that can alter historical events.
  • Delta-2: Nearly impossible to contain due to immense power or chaotic behavior.
  • Examples: Interdimensional portals that can randomly open and close, risking cross-dimensional invasions.
  • Delta-3: Potential bioweapon or mass-destruction capabilities; top-priority containment.
  • Examples: Bio-engineered viruses designed for rapid and lethal outbreaks.

Level Epsilon - Special Cases

  • Epsilon-1: Require unconventional or experimental containment methods.
  • Examples: Living paintings that can trap people within their scenes.
  • Epsilon-2: Symbiotic or intertwined with key personnel, technology, or other Aberrations.
  • Examples: Soul-bonded artifacts that require a human host to function.
  • Epsilon-3: Involving alien technology or cosmic phenomena.
  • Examples: Extraterrestrial devices with unknown functions and potentially catastrophic capabilities.

Level Omega - Beyond Classification

  • Omega-1: Transcend conventional understanding or containment capabilities.
  • Examples: Entities embodying abstract concepts like fear or time.
  • Omega-2: Intertwined with fundamental cosmic principles or dimensions.
  • Examples: Beings from higher-dimensional spaces that can alter physical laws.
  • Omega-3: Connected to the origin or fundamental fabric of the universe; restricted info.
  • Examples: Entities affecting reality itself, potentially rewriting existence.

Level Zeta - Uncontained

  • Zeta-1: Known and monitored but currently uncontainable due to resource or technological limitations.
  • Examples: Wandering spectral entities that appear sporadically and unpredictably.
  • Zeta-2: Actively evading containment efforts, posing significant threat.
  • Examples: Rogue telekinetic individuals who have escaped containment.
  • Zeta-3: Unknown whereabouts; high priority for retrieval and containment.
  • Examples: A sentient nanobot swarm that escaped during a research project.
